What is Dinozards?

Dinozards is a multiplayer arena game where up to four players battle each other free-for-all style. Players have three abilities at their disposal, a fireball, a reflect ability and a dash. Using them and position yourself favorably is the key to victory as the last player standing wins. The game also has a 2v2 mode for epic team battles.

This was a 7 week long school project I was apart of, together with 11 other people. The team consisted of 3 programmers, 3 game designers, 6 artists. Our main goal was to create a simple, yet fun game given the seven week time frame. It’s very easy to overscope a project like this so we chose an idea that would allow plenty of time for polishing.

What did I do during the project?

For this project I mainly worked on the input system, player movement, knockback and damage systems. I also took somewhat of a “lead programmer” role which mostly meant that I was the main person to talk to regarding gameplay systems and our general “code direction” for lack of a better term. 2 out of our 3 designers are also very technical, meaning we had effectively 5 people writing code. To keep the code consistent I wrote a coding standard document so everyone was on the same page.

Link to the Coding Standard document.

Towards the end of the project I spent a lot of time looking at the Unity profiler to see if any performance optimizations could be made. I was mainly looking for unnecessary instances of garbage collections, over use of GetComponent() or cases of high CPU usage and the cause for it.
